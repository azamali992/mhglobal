# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

International apparel brand buyers, importers, wholesalers, and private-label businesses evaluating MH Global Attire (Faisalabad, Pakistan) as a manufacturing partner. They arrive comparing multiple manufacturers/exporters, often from higher-production-value markets (US/EU/Gulf), and are judging manufacturing competence and trustworthiness partly through the quality of the website itself before ever making contact. Primary job to be done: assess capability (fabrics, GSM ranges, customization, QC process, OEM workflow) and submit a detailed product inquiry.

## Product Purpose

A B2B marketing/lead-generation site for a custom apparel manufacturer. It exists to convert cold buyer traffic into qualified inquiries (Request a Quote submissions) by demonstrating manufacturing credibility, process rigor, and product range. Success = buyers trust the company enough to submit a detailed inquiry with product/quantity/fabric specs.

## Brand Personality

Confident, precise, established — the client's own words: "really advanced, professional, extremely attractive." Three words: **precise, confident, editorial.** Should read like a Fortune-500 B2B manufacturer or premium agency site, not a template. Warm-serious register: navy + crimson + cream, serif display headlines paired with a clean sans workhorse body — architectural rather than playful.

## Anti-references

Explicitly NOT: generic Bootstrap/WordPress manufacturer template look, generic SaaS-landing-page tropes (hero-metric stat blocks, gradient-text headlines, tiny uppercase tracked eyebrows above every section, numbered 01/02/03 scaffolding on non-sequential content), stock "corporate handshake" clichés, cluttered/dense B2B-directory-listing density. Must never claim unverified certifications or sustainability programs (client instruction — Sustainability page uses "developing practices" framing only, no achieved-certification claims).

## Design Principles

1. **Editorial confidence over template density** — generous whitespace, strong typographic hierarchy, large intentional imagery; when in doubt, remove a section rather than cram it in.
2. **Every claim must be traceable** — copy comes verbatim from the approved content library or seeded CMS content; nothing invented, no fabricated stats/certifications.
3. **Motion earns its place** — animation should feel expensive and purposeful (Aceternity-inspired components, scroll-triggered reveals with real spring physics), never decorative filler, and must degrade gracefully under `prefers-reduced-motion`.
4. **Locked brand system, not a moving target** — the 9-token palette and type scale from the Phase 0 design system are fixed; new pages compose with existing primitives rather than inventing new ones.
5. **Buyer trust is the metric** — every section should make an international buyer more confident in this manufacturer's competence, not just look good in isolation.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA minimum: body text ≥4.5:1 contrast, large text ≥3:1, visible focus-visible states on every interactive element, semantic landmarks (`<nav>`, `<main>`, `<address>`, `<ol>` for sequential process steps). `prefers-reduced-motion` must be respected by every animated component — static equivalent, not just "animation removed leaving broken layout."
